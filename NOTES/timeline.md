# Epstein Files: Complete Timeline

## The Investigation Begins (2005-2006)

- **March 2005** - Palm Beach police begin investigating Epstein after a 14-year-old's family reports molestation at his mansion. Multiple high school students claim he hired them for "sexual massages."
- **May 2006** - Police prepare charges for multiple counts of unlawful sex with a minor, but State Attorney Barry Krischer sends the case to a grand jury instead.
- **July 2006** - Grand jury indicts Epstein on soliciting prostitution only (a minor charge). Palm Beach police publicly accuse Krischer of favoritism. FBI launches its own investigation ("Operation Leap Year").

## Secret Deal and Light Sentence (2007-2009)

- **2007** - Federal prosecutors draft a 53-page, 60-count indictment against Epstein. Epstein's lawyers negotiate with U.S. Attorney Alexander Acosta (Miami) to avoid federal prosecution. Acosta's office repeatedly stalls the indictment.
- **June 2008** - Epstein pleads guilty to state charges: soliciting prostitution and soliciting prostitution from a minor. Gets 18 months in minimum-security, serves most on work-release. The secret plea deal shields co-conspirators from prosecution.
- **May 2009** - Accuser Virginia Roberts Giuffre sues, claiming Epstein and Ghislaine Maxwell arranged encounters with "royalty, politicians, academicians, businessmen."
- **July 2009** - Epstein released. Accusers spend the next decade fighting to void his federal non-prosecution agreement.

## Media Pressure Builds (2011-2018)

- **March 2, 2011** - Daily Mail publishes Giuffre's interview describing a 2003 encounter with Prince Andrew in London at age 17.
- **Dec. 30, 2014** - Giuffre's lawyers file papers claiming encounters with Prince Andrew, "foreign presidents, a well-known Prime Minister, and other world leaders." All deny allegations.
- **November 2018** - Miami Herald's investigative series revisits Epstein's case handling, highlighting Acosta's role (now Trump's labor secretary). Public outrage intensifies.
- **Dec. 6, 2018** - FBI agents and Manhattan U.S. Attorney's office begin a fresh investigation.

## New York Prosecution (2019)

- **July 6, 2019** - Epstein arrested on federal sex trafficking charges by SDNY prosecutors (not bound by Florida's non-prosecution agreement). Acosta resigns days later.
- **Aug. 10, 2019** - Epstein dies by hanging in his Manhattan jail cell. NYC medical examiner rules suicide. Widespread public skepticism. Guards were asleep; cameras malfunctioned.

## Maxwell Trial (2020-2022)

- **July 2, 2020** - Ghislaine Maxwell charged with sex crimes for recruiting and abusing victims.
- **Dec. 30, 2021** - Jury convicts Maxwell of sex trafficking and related crimes after month-long trial.
- **June 28, 2022** - Maxwell sentenced to 20 years.

## Renewed Public Interest (2024-2025)

- **January 2024** - Judge releases more court records from related civil lawsuit. Public interest surges.
- **Jan. 20, 2025** - Trump inaugurated; had suggested during 2024 campaign he'd open more government files.
- **February 2025** - AG Pam Bondi mentions an Epstein "client list" on Fox News. DOJ distributes "declassified" binders to far-right influencers (mostly already-public info).
- **April 25, 2025** - Virginia Giuffre dies by suicide.
- **July 7, 2025** - DOJ states Epstein maintained no "client list" and won't release additional files voluntarily.
- **July 15, 2025** - Reps. Ro Khanna (D-CA) and Thomas Massie (R-KY) introduce the **Epstein Files Transparency Act** (H.R. 4405).
- **July 17, 2025** - Wall Street Journal reports a suggestive letter bearing Trump's name from Epstein's 2003 birthday album. Trump denies writing it and sues WSJ.
- **July 24-25, 2025** - Deputy AG Todd Blanche interviews Maxwell. She denies wrongdoing, claims never witnessing Trump in "sexually inappropriate activity." She's transferred to a Texas minimum-security facility.

## Royal Consequences (Oct-Nov 2025)

- **Oct. 21, 2025** - Giuffre's posthumous memoir published, revisiting trafficking allegations against Epstein, Maxwell, and Prince Andrew.
- **Oct. 30, 2025** - King Charles III strips Prince Andrew of remaining titles and evicts him from royal residence.
- **Nov. 12, 2025** - House committee releases email correspondence between Epstein and various figures (Prince Andrew, Steve Bannon, Larry Summers, Reid Hoffman). An Epstein email states Trump "knew about the girls."
- **Nov. 14, 2025** - At Trump's urging, Bondi announces Manhattan U.S. Attorney will investigate Epstein's connections to Trump's political opponents (Clinton, Summers, Hoffman). None face accusations from actual accusers.

## Epstein Files Transparency Act (Nov-Dec 2025)

- **Nov. 18, 2025** - House passes the Act 427-1. Senate unanimously approves same day.
- **Nov. 19, 2025** - Trump signs the Epstein Files Transparency Act into law (Public Law 119-38).
- **Dec. 19, 2025** - DOJ begins releasing records (the legal deadline). Release includes photos of Epstein with famous people. DOJ quickly halts, citing need for further review. Over 500 pages entirely blacked out. Bipartisan criticism for failing to meet the law's requirements.

## The Major Dump (Jan-Feb 2026)

- **Jan. 30, 2026** - DOJ releases 3+ million pages, 2,000 videos, 180,000 images. Deputy AG Blanche calls this the final release, bringing total to ~3.5 million pages. DOJ claims compliance with the Act. Only ~6 million total pages may qualify, but DOJ says it's done.
- **Feb. 1, 2026** - Attorneys for 200+ alleged victims ask federal judges to take down the DOJ Epstein website, calling it "the single most egregious violation of victim privacy in one day in United States history." DOJ had failed to redact names of victims and individuals not previously publicized. DOJ also accidentally published unredacted nude images of young women/possible teens.
- **Feb. 3, 2026** - NPR reports DOJ failed to properly redact names of publicly identified victims across many documents.
- **Feb. 9, 2026** - Members of Congress allowed to view unredacted files in a DOJ reading room. No electronic devices allowed; notes only.
- **Feb. 10, 2026** - Rep. Ro Khanna reads 6 names from unredacted files on the House floor, says they are "likely incriminated." Includes billionaire Leslie Wexner (labeled FBI co-conspirator in 2019). Deputy AG Blanche disputes, calling them "completely random people."
- **Feb. 11, 2026** - DOJ removes bulk ZIP download links from the Epstein Library website. Files now accessible only as individual PDFs one at a time.
- **Feb. 12, 2026** - Goldman Sachs CLO Kathryn Ruemmler announces resignation (effective June 30, 2026) after files show she was one of three people Epstein called after his 2019 arrest.
- **Feb. 14, 2026** - NPR reports Epstein files fallout is taking down elite figures in Europe while the U.S. reckoning remains muted.
